Our Story

Three generations at the same wheel

Aman Studio began in a one-room workshop where Aman's grandfather taught the family trade — turning riverbed clay into kalash pots for weddings and diyas for Diwali. The wheel still spins in the same courtyard.

Today we work with a small collective of artisan families across Bishnupur, keeping the same hand-building and pit-firing techniques alive, just reaching further than the village market now does.

No two pieces leave our kiln looking the same — the iron in the clay, the ash in the fire, and the hand of the maker all leave their mark.

Aman Kumar
Founder & Master Potter

How It's Made

From riverbank to your shelf

Dig & Knead

Sourcing the clay

Clay is hand-dug from local riverbeds, sun-dried, soaked, and kneaded to remove air and stones.

Throw & Shape

The wheel

Each form is thrown by hand on a manual kick-wheel — no moulds, so every piece has its own quiet variation.

Carve & Dry

Etching & detailing

Motifs are carved while the clay is leather-hard, then left to air-dry slowly for up to a week.

Fire & Finish

The kiln

Pieces are pit-fired at low heat for that signature warm, unglazed terracotta tone.
